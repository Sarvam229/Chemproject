1. PRODUCT INTRODUCTION
Azigo 500 is a brand name for "Azithromycin 500 mg", which is commonly used to treat bacterial infections.
It belongs to the class of "macrolide antibiotics" and is widely prescribed for a variety of infections. Below is a comprehensive guide to "Azigo 500".

2. Drugs Present
  • Active Ingredient: Azithromycin (500 mg per tablet or dose).
  • Excipients: The specific inactive ingredients may vary depending on the formulation (tablet, suspension).
    They are typically fillers or binders that help in the formulation and stability of the drug.

3. When to Use Azigo 500
Azigo 500 (Azithromycin) is used for treating infections caused by bacteria. These may include:
  • Respiratory tract infections: Bronchitis, pneumonia, pharyngitis (throat infections), tonsillitis.
  • Ear infections: Otitis media.
  • Skin and soft tissue infections: Including cellulitis, impetigo.
  • Sexually transmitted infections (STIs): Chlamydia, gonorrhea.
  • Gastrointestinal infections: Travelerâ€™s diarrhea (caused by susceptible organisms).
  • Other: Azigo may also be prescribed for conditions like sinusitis, Lyme disease, and certain infections caused by *Mycobacterium avium (in immunocompromised patients).
4. How to Use Azigo 500
  • Dosage Form: Azigo 500 is typically available in tablet form, and it may also come in a suspension for those who have difficulty swallowing pills.
  • Dosage Instructions: The typical adult dosage is:
    • 500 mg on the first day, followed by 250 mg daily for the next 4 days.
    • For some infections, a single dose of 1 gram (1000 mg) may be given, such as for chlamydia or gonorrhea.
    • Children: Dosage for children is based on their weight, and a doctor will determine the exact amount
  • Timing: It can be taken with or without food.
  • Missed Doses: If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at once.
5. Side Effects

Most side effects of Azigo 500 are mild, but some can be serious. Common and less common side effects include:
  • Common:
    • Diarrhea
    • Nausea or vomiting
    • Stomach pain or discomfort
    • Headache
    • Fatigue or drowsiness
  • Serious(seek medical attention if any of these occur):
    • Severe allergic reactions: Rash, itching, swelling of the face/lips/tongue, difficulty breathing.
    • Liver problems: Jaundice (yellowing of the skin or eyes), dark urine, abdominal pain.
    • Heart problems: Irregular heartbeat, palpitations, or chest pain (QT prolongation).
    • Severe diarrhea: Can indicate Clostridium difficile infection, which can be serious.
    • Vision problems: Temporary vision changes or blurred vision in rare cases.
6. Relief Time
  • Onset of Action: Azigo 500 starts working relatively quickly, with many patients feeling improvement within 24 to 48 hours of starting the medication.
  • Full Recovery: While relief may begin within a couple of days, it is important to complete the full prescribed course , even if symptoms improve before the medication is finished,
    to fully eradicate the infection and prevent resistance.

7. Warnings and Precautions
  • Allergy: Do not use Azigo if you are allergic to azithromycin or any other macrolide antibiotics (e.g., erythromycin).
  • Liver Issues: Use with caution if you have liver disease, as it may affect liver function.
  • Heart Conditions: Azigo can prolong the QT interval and may cause arrhythmias. Patients with a history of heart problems or electrolyte imbalances (low potassium or magnesium)
    should use Azigo cautiously.
  • Pregnancy: Azithromycin is generally considered safe for use during pregnancy (Category B), but it should only be used when clearly needed. Always consult your healthcare provider.
  • Breastfeeding: Azithromycin passes into breast milk in small amounts, but is usually considered safe for breastfeeding mothers. However, consult your doctor.
  • Diabetes: The liquid form of azithromycin may contain sugar, which could affect blood glucose levels.

Summary
Azigo 500 (Azithromycin) is an effective antibiotic for treating a wide range of bacterial infections. It is typically well-tolerated, but as with all antibiotics,
it is important to take it exactly as prescribed. Be aware of potential side effects, especially related to heart or liver function, and interact with your healthcare provider
if you are on any other medications or have pre-existing conditions. Always complete the full course of treatment, even if symptoms improve early, to avoid the risk of the infection
returning or developing antibiotic resistance.
